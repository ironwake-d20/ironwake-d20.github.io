# What Is a Character?

An Ironwake character is defined by several interconnected systems. This page introduces each at a conceptual level. Detailed rules follow in later chapters.

## Attributes

Six core attributes define a character's raw capabilities: **Strength**, **Dexterity**, **Constitution**, **Intelligence**, **Wisdom**, and **Charisma**. Each typically ranges from 6 to 18 (for starting characters) and produces a modifier used in checks. The average human has all of them at 10.

## Class

A class is a calling, not a profession. It requires sufficient **kai** to manifest. The four classes are Fighter, Healer, Spellcaster, and Rogue. Most people in the world have no class.

## Kai

Kai is the innate capacity for class-level ability — the depth of the well. It is fixed at the point of awakening and cannot be trained into existence. Your kai tier sets the ceiling on how far you can progress.

## Mana

Mana is the spendable resource for kai-driven abilities — the water in the well. Spellcasters and healers draw from it constantly; fighters and rogues use it only through specific subclasses. Mana recovers with time only.

## Vitality and Wounds

Durability has two layers. **Vitality** represents stamina, near-misses, and armour absorption — it depletes freely in combat. **Wounds** represent actual anatomical damage — a fixed, low number that is hard to recover.

## Skills and feats

Skills represent training and expertise. Feats represent special capabilities — combat techniques, magical mastery, or general talents — that distinguish your character from others of the same class.
