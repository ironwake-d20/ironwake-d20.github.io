# Conditions

Conditions are ongoing effects that alter a character's capabilities. They are imposed by wounds, spells, environmental hazards, and other circumstances.

## Condition list

### Bleeding

Ongoing Vitality loss each round until treated. Caused by certain wound types (throat/neck hits).

### Concussion

Disadvantage on Intelligence and Wisdom checks. Caused by head wounds. Persists until treated.

### Dying

When a character's Wounds are reduced to zero, they are dying. They must make stabilisation checks each round. Three failures = death. Three successes = stabilised.

### Prone

A prone character has disadvantage on attack rolls. Melee attacks against them have advantage; ranged attacks have disadvantage. Standing up costs half your movement.

### Silenced

Cannot speak or produce verbal components for spells. Caused by throat/neck wounds.

### Stabilised

No longer dying, but unconscious and at zero Wounds. Will regain consciousness after 1d4 hours.

### Stunned

Cannot take actions or reactions. Attacks against a stunned creature have advantage.

### Unconscious

Cannot take actions or reactions. Drops whatever is held. Falls prone. Attacks against an unconscious creature have advantage and are automatic critical hits if within 5 feet.

### Winded

Disadvantage on Strength and Constitution checks. Caused by abdomen wounds. Persists until treated.
