# Overview

This rulebook describes a d20 system designed for dark fantasy settings. It is mechanically self-contained but assumes a world with the following properties:

- The capacity for power is unevenly distributed, either **at birth** or at an **awakening event**.
- That capacity is **innate and fixed**: it can be measured, degraded, but not trained into existence
- There are superpowers, that are trying to control who possess it. They may be **religious organisations**, **tech giants**, **government alliances**

What the cosmology calls this capacity, which god sanctions it, and what the powerful institutions look like are yours to decide. The mechanics are indifferent to those answers.

---

## Design principles

**Narrative accuracy over abstraction.** Combat mechanics try to reflect how battles and duels actually worked — accumulated degradation, decisive finishing strikes, precise bypasses of armour. Numbers should feel like something.

!!! tip "GM note"

    Narrative accuracy is meant to improve the immersion of the game. It does not mean that the game should be realistic; it's still fantasy. Also, it's meant to remove the over-reliance in the calculations.


**Fixed ceilings, meaningful choices within them.** A character's innate capacity sets their ceiling. Everything else is how they spend what they have.

**No moral champions.** All characters — player and non-player — are morally grey. The system does not reward or punish alignment. [Pacts] exist, and their tragedy comes from knowing the price and paying it anyway.

!!! tip "GM note"

    Morally grey shouldn't be thought as "everyone is chaotic neutral", instead it is "everyone has their own agenda, and they are willing to do morally questionable things to achieve it". Similarly, even malevolent forces may have their own code, for example a demon may consider honestly and loyalty unbreakable.

**Portable core, honest defaults.** The system ships with a medievalpunk setting as its default expression. Subclass names, institutional names, and cosmological flavour can be reskinned. The one hard requirement is a fiction that explains why some individuals have class potential and others do not.

!!! tip "GM note"

    This game is designed to be a toolbox for fantasy settings. The default setting is a medievalpunk world, but the mechanics are designed to be flexible enough to support a variety of settings. While the default
    assumes that the institutional religion and a few major churches are the world's primary powers, you can replace them with tech giants, government alliances, or anything else you can imagine.
