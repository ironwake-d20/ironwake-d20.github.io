# Overview

This rulebook describes a d20 system designed for dark fantasy settings. It is mechanically self-contained but assumes a world with the following properties:

- The capacity for power is **cosmic in origin** and unevenly distributed at birth
- That capacity is **innate and fixed** — it can be measured, degraded, but not trained into existence
- Institutional religion has **political interest** in controlling those who possess it
- Tragedy emerges from **known prices paid**, not from betrayal or corrupted wishes

What the cosmology calls this capacity, which god sanctions it, and what the Church looks like are yours to decide. The mechanics are indifferent to those answers.

---

## Design principles

**Narrative accuracy over abstraction.** Combat mechanics reflect how medieval violence actually worked — accumulated degradation, decisive finishing strikes, precise bypasses of armour. Numbers should feel like something.

**Fixed ceilings, meaningful choices within them.** A character's innate capacity sets their ceiling. Everything else is how they spend what they have.

**No moral champions.** All characters — player and non-player — are morally grey. The system does not reward alignment. Pacts exist, and their tragedy comes from knowing the price and paying it anyway.

**Portable core, honest defaults.** The system ships with a medievalpunk setting as its default expression. Subclass names, institutional names, and cosmological flavour can be reskinned. The one hard requirement is a fiction that explains why some individuals have class potential and others do not.
