# Classes

## What a class is

A class is a calling, not a profession. It requires sufficient kai to manifest — without innate capacity, techniques can be learned but no class levels, bonuses, or kai-driven features are accessible. The vast majority of the population has no class.

Class-bearers are treated by institutional religion as assets first and people second. This is not flavour — it has mechanical consequences in social encounters, legal standing, and the attention of the Church's enforcement arms.

---

## Multiclassing

Multiclass characters do not exist under normal circumstances. A character has one class. Subclasses allow meaningful specialisation within that class.

Extraordinary circumstances — divine intervention, exposure to a cosmic event, a sufficiently costly pact — may produce a second class. These cases are narrative events adjudicated by the GM, not character build options.

---

## The four classes

| Class       | Core drive                            | Mana use                     |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Fighter     | Martial mastery, physical dominance   | Subclass-dependent only      |
| Healer      | Restoration, support, kai channelling | Active; subclass-dependent   |
| Spellcaster | School-based magic, kai shaping       | Active; central to the class |
| Rogue       | Precision, deception, opportunity     | Subclass-dependent only      |

Fighters and Rogues do not spend mana as a baseline class mechanic. Their kai-limited features (precision techniques, favoured enemy counts, channel capacities) are bounded by kai tier but do not draw from a mana pool unless a specific subclass introduces active resource expenditure.

---

## Subclasses

A character may acquire subclasses during levels 4–13, gaining one subclass level per level in that range (10 subclass levels total). Subclasses are chosen by the player.

A character has **one main class** and may have **multiple subclasses**, provided all subclasses are compatible with the main class. A Fighter may be a Paladin/Berserker. A Fighter may not simultaneously be a Healer.

Subclass features are kai-limited in the same way as main class features.

### Fighter subclasses

| Subclass   | Notes                                                                                                                                                      |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Berserker  | Rage-driven combat; offensive focus                                                                                                                        |
| Paladin    | Faith-driven combat; defensive and support features                                                                                                        |
| Inquisitor | Intelligence-based fighter; perception and interrogation features. Reskinnable — a secular equivalent exists under different names in non-Church contexts. |
| Ranger     | Favoured enemy system; kai limits enemy count                                                                                                              |

## Kai requirements by class

Minimum kai to manifest a class is 1,000. No player character operates near this threshold — the practical floor for play is Forge tier (4,000+).

The kai tier sets the **level ceiling** for all class and subclass features. A Forge-tier character (4,000–4,999) can reach approximately level 17 regardless of class. A higher-tier character reaches further; a lower-tier character hits their ceiling earlier.

## Imbued artefacts

Spellcasters can imbue objects with kai, producing artefacts that allow non-class-bearers to use spell effects. These are wielded by common people who want to play the hero — often without the ending they would wish for.

Imbued artefacts are single-use or charge-limited. They do not grant a class. They do not respond to the wielder's kai (they have none). They behave exactly as the spellcaster who made them intended — no more, no less.
