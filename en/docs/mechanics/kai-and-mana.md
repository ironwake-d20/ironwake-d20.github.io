# Kai & Mana

## What kai is

Kai is the innate capacity for class-level ability. In the default setting it originates from stellar radiation — exploding stars emit kai, and some individuals absorb it at a formative stage rather than letting it pass through like background radiation. It leaves physical residue, can be measured with instruments, and does not grant miracles.

The fictional explanation for kai's origin is reskinnable. The mechanical requirement is not: **some individuals have it, most do not, and the amount is fixed at the point of awakening**. It cannot be trained into existence.

Kai is the **depth of the well**. Mana is the water.

## Kai tiers

| Range       | Tier          | Max level | Profile                                                                                                |
| ----------- | ------------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1–999       | Inert         | No class  | The vast majority of the population. Kai is present but functionally irrelevant. ~95% of people.       |
| 1,000–1,999 | Spark         | ~5        | Detectable. Enough to manifest a class, barely. These individuals are registered and monitored.        |
| 2,000–2,999 | Ember         | ~8        | Minor local talent. A meaningful career is possible — guild enforcer, village healer, court artificer. |
| 3,000–3,999 | Flame         | ~13       | Regional renown possible. The Church takes these individuals seriously.                                |
| 4,000–4,999 | Forge         | ~17       | Standard player character range. Acts that become local legend.                                        |
| 5,000–5,999 | Blaze         | ~20       | Standard hero tier. A full campaign arc. Named in chronicles; remembered for generations.              |
| 6,000–6,999 | Pyre          | ~20+      | Rare enough that most people never meet one. Changes the outcomes of wars.                             |
| 7,000–7,999 | Inferno       | ~20+      | A handful per generation, globally. Destabilises political structures.                                 |
| 8,000–8,999 | Conflagration | ~20+      | Generational hero. Their awakening may have astronomical correlates.                                   |
| 10,000+     | Stellar       | Uncapped  | Only a handful in recorded history. The Church has no doctrine for them — only silence.                |

No player character begins below Forge (4,000). The 1,000 class threshold is real but no one at the table operates near it.

### Intra-tier differences

Within a tier, the exact kai value produces small differences felt at character creation and in non-combat play only.

Each tier is divided into three bands:

| Band | Position  | Effect                                                                |
| ---- | --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Low  | x000–x299 | Base skill points for tier                                            |
| Mid  | x300–x699 | +2 bonus skill points at creation                                     |
| High | x700–x999 | +2 bonus skill points + +1 to one class-relevant skill check category |

The +1 skill check bonus is chosen from a short list tied to the character's class. It is never a free +1 to any skill.

### Choosing a kai value

Players choose a tier appropriate to the campaign, then either roll (1d10 × 100 + 1d100 − 1) or pick a number within the tier. The number may be deferred — fixed narratively when it first matters, such as when the Church measures the character or an artificer reads their residue.

## Mana

Mana is the spendable resource for all kai-driven abilities across all classes. It is not exclusive to spellcasters — a fighter's precision techniques, a ranger's favoured enemy count, a healer's channel capacity all draw from the same pool where applicable.

!!! note "Class variation"

    Not all classes use mana as an active resource. Fighters and rogues spend mana only through specific subclasses. Base class features for these classes are kai-limited but not mana-gated.

### Mana pool

```
Max mana = (10 × caster level) + (Intelligence modifier × caster level)
```

A level 10 character with Intelligence 18 (+4 modifier) has a mana pool of 140.

Intelligence therefore serves double duty: it gates maximum spell level (see [Magic](magic.md)) and amplifies the mana pool. It is the primary stat for spellcasters without being the only one that matters.

### Recovery

Mana recovers with **time only**. There are no shortcuts in the base rules — no meditation, no potions, no ley line tapping. A wounded character on forced march still recovers, just more slowly.

Recovery rate scales with kai depth. A Blaze-tier character recovers meaningfully overnight. A Forge-tier character may need two or three days to feel full after serious expenditure.

!!! note "GM's discretion"

    External mana sources (potions, ley lines, rituals) are setting-specific and not assumed by the base rules. GMs introducing them should treat them as significant and rare.

## Overdraw

A character may spend past zero mana. The threshold is perceptible — they feel it before they cross it. Crossing it is a deliberate choice.

Overdraw damage lands on the **kai layer**, not the wound system. It is not physical trauma. Healers, surgeons, and medicine cannot touch it.

Each overdraw event permanently reduces the kai ceiling by a small amount, which in turn slightly shrinks the mana maximum and may reduce kai-limited feature counts. Repeated overdraw is a slow self-erasure — a spellcaster who burns out habitually is gradually becoming someone without a class.

Overdraw damage does not recover under normal circumstances. Extraordinary narrative intervention — potentially including a pact — may be the only recourse.

!!! warning "Pact intersection"

    A spellcaster at zero mana facing certain death is exactly the conditions under which pact granters are drawn. The price of one last overdraw paid permanently in kai is structurally coherent with the pact system. See [Pacts](./pacts.md).
