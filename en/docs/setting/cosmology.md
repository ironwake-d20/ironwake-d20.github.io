# Cosmology

The cosmology of Ironwake explains where kai comes from, what pact granters might be, and what exists beyond the material world. The rules specify enough to make the mechanics coherent; the rest is left for the GM.

## The origin of kai

In the default setting, kai originates from stellar radiation. Exploding stars emit kai, and some individuals absorb it at a formative stage rather than letting it pass through like background radiation. It leaves physical residue, can be measured with instruments, and does not grant miracles.

This explanation is reskinnable. The mechanical requirement is not the stellar origin — it is that some individuals have kai, most do not, and the amount is fixed at the point of awakening.

## Pact granters

Pact granters are entities drawn to despair. They are not named, statted, or fully described in the base rules. What they are, what they want, why they respond — these are setting questions.

What the rules specify:

- They exist
- They listen
- They are honest
- They are not merciful

Possible interpretations include: remnants of previous stellar events, entities from beyond the material plane, fragments of dead class-bearers whose kai persisted after death, or something the Church's doctrine cannot account for.

## The unseen world

Beyond the material world, the rules acknowledge that something exists. Pact granters come from somewhere. Kai comes from somewhere. The Church has doctrine about both — and the doctrine is incomplete.

The rules deliberately leave these questions open. The GM decides what is true in their world. The mechanics only require that the answers are consistent with: kai is innate and fixed, pact granters are drawn to despair, and the Church has political interest in controlling both.
