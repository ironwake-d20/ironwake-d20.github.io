# Imbued Artefacts

Spellcasters can imbue objects with kai, producing artefacts that allow non-class-bearers to use spell effects. These are wielded by common people who want to play the hero — often without the ending they would wish for.

## What an imbued artefact is

An imbued artefact is a physical object that stores a spell effect. It is created by a spellcaster who permanently invests a portion of their mana into the object. The artefact can then be activated by anyone — class-bearer or not — to produce the stored effect.

## Properties

- **Single-use or charge-limited.** Artefacts do not produce infinite effects.
- **No scaling.** The artefact behaves exactly as the spellcaster who made it intended. It does not respond to the wielder's kai (they have none).
- **No class granted.** Using an artefact does not make you a class-bearer.

## Degradation and fracture

Artefacts are not permanent. Over time, or with overuse, they degrade. A fractured artefact may release its stored magic unpredictably.
