# Armour & Shields

Armour does not reduce raw damage — it reduces injury **severity**. A hit that reaches Wounds against a heavily armoured opponent rolls the injury die at disadvantage.

## Armour tiers

| Tier | Example          | Injury die                                 | Stealth      | STR req. |
| ---- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------ | -------- |
| 0    | Unarmoured       | Normal roll                                | —            | —        |
| 1    | Padded / leather | Normal roll                                | —            | —        |
| 2    | Mail             | Roll twice, take lower                     | Disadvantage | 13       |
| 3    | Plate            | Roll twice, take lower; Minor on 1–4       | Disadvantage | 15       |
| 4    | Full plate       | Roll twice, take lower; Grievous only on 6 | Disadvantage | 17       |

## Shields

A shield provides a bonus to defence. It can also be used as an improvised weapon (d4 bludgeoning).

## Donning and doffing

| Armour type      | Don        | Doff      |
| ---------------- | ---------- | --------- |
| Light (tier 1)   | 1 minute   | 1 minute  |
| Medium (tier 2)  | 5 minutes  | 1 minute  |
| Heavy (tier 3–4) | 10 minutes | 5 minutes |
| Shield           | 1 action   | 1 action  |

## Armour maintenance

Armour degrades with use. After significant combat, armour may require repair. Damaged armour provides reduced protection.
